# Cheddar

Cheddar is the broader application and branding line for practical life-organization and money-related tools.

It sits above any one specific app and above any one specific business workflow.

Cheddar is where the user-facing product family gathers when the purpose is to help real people manage everyday life, money, and organization more clearly without surrendering privacy or paying unnecessary SaaS costs.

## Current Shape

Current surfaced directions include:

- `CheddarBooks`
  the small-business and recordkeeping division under Cheddar
- `CheddarMoney`
  a broader personal-finance and money-visibility direction that has already surfaced in the imported discussions
- other practical log-style and recordkeeping tools that may still need clearer placement

## Relationship To CheddarBooks

CheddarBooks is not the whole of Cheddar.

CheddarBooks is one division under it, focused on micro-business and small-business bookkeeping, recordkeeping, and related support flows.

See:

- [`../cheddarbooks-foundation.md`](../cheddarbooks-foundation.md)
- [`../cheddarbooks/README.md`](../cheddarbooks/README.md)

## Relationship To NEXUS

Cheddar uses the NEXUS and FnHCI/FnUI foundations.

It is not itself the platform.

That means:

- NEXUS remains the foundation workspace
- FnHCI/FnUI remains the interaction and runtime platform line
- Cheddar remains one family of concrete user-facing products built on top

## Current Direction

The current product direction grounded in the imported history is:

- practical and personally useful first
- privacy-conscious by default
- friendly to self-hosting and owner control where that matters
- open and inspectable where the rights and handling boundaries allow it
